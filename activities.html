What to do

Onsen

One of the main things you have to do when you visit Japan is to visit an onsen. Onsens are natural springs fueled by volcanoes that are located all over japan. In japanese culture the hot spring water is said to cure sickness. It is a perfect to relax and let the jet lag go away.


  • Price: 7,000yen- 11,000yen
  • Location: All around Japan
  • Age group: 7yro and older

Matsumoto Castle

Matsumoto castle is one of the most beautiful and still standing castles in Japan. It is connected to a cherry blossom garden that you can walk around and lead you into the castle where you will be able to explore all the inner sorroundings.


  • Price: 700yen
  • Location: City Matsumoto
  • Age group: All ages

Mario Go-Kart

Do you want to explore the city of Tokyo and race your friends and family at the same. You should give Mario Karts guides a go. Tours last anywhere from 1 hour to 3 hours. You'll follow a guide through the city of Tokyo while sight seeing different local spots. Though you will need a valid driver license.


  • Price: 9,000yen- 13,000yen
  • Location: Tokyo
  • Age group: 18 and older

Universal Studios

Universal studios is always a must no matter what country you visit. Though in Japan is a bit different and more exciting. This universal studios has different themes influenced by popular pop culture and anime culture. Besides all the rides and fun roller coasters, you can also visit Naruto's hidden leaf village, or be in the middle of the action fighting a titan from Attack on Titan. Universal studios is a great opportunity to spend time with your family and friends.


  • Price: 7,400yen for adults - 5,400 yen for children
  • Location: Osaka
  • Age group: All ages

Sakura Matsuri Festival

The sakura matsuri is a street festival that happens all through spring but it is mainly determined by the cherry blossom forecast. Visitors can walk around different areas of Kyoto which are surrounded by cherry blossoms. There are also various performances in the streets all thorughout the day. Though it is common to just sit down and have a picnic with your family and just enjoy the view since cherry blossoms only last two weeks. This is symbolic and connected to the realization that "noting lasts forever".

  • Price: Free
  • Location: All over Japan
  • Age group: All ages

Akihabara street

Akihabara is famous for all of its electronics store. And in recent years has seen a gain in recognition due to the huge anime and manga fanbase. Akihabara is the best place to grab souvenires for family and friends as well as expieriencing the growing anime culture and its community.


  • Price: varies
  • Location: Tokyo
