What to do

Food Tour

Japan offers a huge variety of foods and sometimes picking what to eat can become diffuclt, even more in large goups of people. Thats why different companies such as Arigato Japan offer food tours. You will be visiting different hidden foods known to locals as well as the more popular ones, all while sighting what Japan has to offer.


  • Price: $125-$250
  • Location: Tokyo, Akihabara, Osaka

Azumaya Soba

Azumaya soba serves the best wanko soba in the area of Osaka. Wanko Soba is an amazing and fun eating experience. It concists of being served small bowls of soba noodles, and immediately after you finish the bowl your server will fill it up again. This will continue until you can't eat anymore. This makes for a fun competition with friends and family. The record for the most bowls eaten is 570! Are you up for the challange. Azumaya Soba also serves a variety of different dishes you can choose from.


  • Price: 2,000yen- 4,3000yen per person
  • Location: Morioka

Uobei Sushi

Are you balling on a budget? or do you want something quick and easy? Uobei sushi is hugely popular restaurant in Japan. It implements Japan's innovation into a restaurant setting. You order your food on a table that is set on your table. Food is delivered via conveyor belt. It's quick, cheap and delicious.


  • Price: 100yen per plate
  • Location: Shibuya

Gas station/Convinience Stores

Though it sounds strange, convinience store food in Japan is nothing like in America. The meals are prepared and kept fresh and ready to eat for a quick bite on the go. It is very common to have them as regular meals through out then day.


  • Price: 100yen- 700yen
  • Location: All over Japan

Ichiran Ramen

Get ready to eat the best ramen Japan has to offer. Ichiran ramen offers a variety of ramen bowls wiht the most popular being Tonkotsu Ramen. Be ready because the lines tend be long any time past lunch hour.


  • Price: 1,200yen- 2,000yen
  • Location: Tokyo

Ameyoko Market

This is the best place to visit if you want to try foods from all over Japan. Back in World War II it served as a balck market but now it serves as host for foods from China, Korea, and all over Japan. From simple ramen dishes to sea food bowls, and sweets and desserts.


  • Price: 300yen- 1,500yen per person
  • Location: Tokyo
